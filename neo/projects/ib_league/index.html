Mind Games

In the category aptly named mind games, contestants' general aptitude for rational and outside-of-the-box thinking will be tested. Facing a diverse choice of questions, the challengers will be encouraged to work together with their teammates to come up with unique solutions for an assortment of questions. For the studious, we recommend taking a look at the study guide and searching for general ability questions online.

This category will be comprised of many groups each with 1-3 members. An exam of 10 questions will be taken by all groups spontaneously. The one with the most correct answers will be declared the winner. In case of a tie additional questions with higher difficulties will be asked. Keep an open mind!! ;)

Starts at 20 June 4:00 P.M.

Science

Our science category will consist of minimum 5 groups of 1-3 students each. For this category, we highly recommend that you attend with a group of 3 people, each from different science classes. If not, that is still appropriate in term of formality and answering the questions.

The group which hasn't been eliminated, in other words, which has survived the league and has taken the 1st place will be rewarded by our team. The study guide for the science category which includes sample questions and curriculum limitations will be published soon in our website. Have fun!!

General Knowledge

Our general knowledge category will include many questions asking odd pieces of information from daily life. It will also have questions about geography, entertainment, history, and trivia in general. You will adore this category if you trust your long-term memory and your collection of knowledge.

Similar to the mind games category, this one will have as many groups as necessary each consisting of at most 5 members. If you desire a better chance at winning, you should check out the study guide and look up some general trivia questions online. Enjoy!

Starts at 20 June 4:00 P.M.

Debate

The Debate Category of the IB League 2021 is the category where IB students from all around the world gather and communicate directly, discussing about global issues, moral values, or daily life problems with using appropriate and formal English language. This category's aim is that students become critical thinkers, caring individuals, aware people who know the significance of the phenomena discussed, and qualified debators.

Our debate category will consist of minimum 8 groups of 2-4 students each. The group which hasn't been eliminated, in other words, which has survived the league and has taken the 1st place will be rewarded by our team. The study guide for the debate category which includes sample debate topics and details about the speeches will be published soon in our website. Have fun!!

Starts at 20 June 4:00 P.M.
